The Capacity-Pressure Effect

Your hiring quality is not fixed. It is a direct output of your operational capacity. Conventional models assume assessment accuracy is a static property, but it is a dynamic variable subject to real-world pressures.

The Core Insight: Overloaded interviewers make worse decisions. When workload passes a critical threshold ($\mathbf{u_{thr}}$), classification accuracy collapses, leading to systemic failure (saturation).

Capacity is not a cost center; it is your primary quality control mechanism.

Operational Physics

The non-linear impact of Utilisation ($\mathbf{u}$) on decision quality. Quality is stable under sustainable load ($\mathbf{u < u_{thr}}$), but degrades rapidly when overloaded.

Cascading Failure

A capacity shortfall in an early, high-volume stage (like CV Screening) degrades its True Negative Rate (TNR), flooding downstream high-cost stages and causing a systemic quality collapse.

FP-Averse Policy (k-Factor)

Organizations often prioritize avoiding a Bad Hire (False Positive, FP) over missing Good Talent (False Negative, FN). The 'Over-cautiousness k' parameter models this intentional risk aversion.
